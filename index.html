Bitcoin

Summary

Bitcoin is the world's first decentralized cryptocurrency. Launched in 2009, it leverages cryptography and peer-to-peer networking to facilitate secure online transactions without the need for intermediaries like banks. Transactions are publicly recorded on a distributed ledger called a blockchain, ensuring transparency and immutability. Bitcoin's value is determined by market forces and has exhibited significant volatility.
